There are certain tools an ways to optimize the skill ceiling and give better results. A good one is using Key frames and such.

These will give a better representation of what you want to do and what movement make appear more appealing to the audience.

Breakdown Poses

These are the last things you should draw.
There are often used to smoothen the animation and make it slower.
Most of time these are not necesary as they are even optional most of the time.

Keys

These are the  very first drawings you should do in order to not only have a clear idea of the placement of your object but to also communicate to the audience what frames they should be seeing first and are more relevant to the movement.

Extreme Poses

These poses are in beetweens to intensify the movement and not make it look too jerky.
Often these should be in groups of 2s or 4s in order to make it more appealing.
